Privacy Policy

Effective date: 7 May 2026  |  Version 1.0

⚠️ DiabEasy processes health data — including blood glucose readings, insulin logs, and medication records — which is classified as special category data under GDPR Article 9. This document explains exactly what we collect, why, how long we keep it, and your rights.

1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

DiabEasy is developed and operated by Liviu Cozac ("we", "us", "our"), an independent developer based in Romania, European Union.

Contact: liviu.dev.cozac@proton.me

For any privacy-related enquiries, including requests to exercise your rights, please contact us at the above address. We will respond within 30 days.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Data You Provide Directly

  • Account information: email address and password (hashed) when you create an account.
  • Profile information: name, date of birth, diabetes type (Type 1, Type 2, gestational, other), preferred glucose unit (mg/dL or mmol/L), preferred language.
  • Health data: blood glucose readings (value, timestamp, measurement type), insulin doses (type, units, timestamp), meal logs (food name, carbohydrates, calories, macros), medication reminders, and notes you choose to enter.
  • Device pairing: Bluetooth device identifiers when you pair a glucometer (e.g., Accu-Chek Instant). No raw Bluetooth data is shared with third parties.
  • Caregiver codes: if you generate or use a caregiver access code, the code itself and the linked account identifier are stored.
  • Emergency contacts: names and phone numbers you enter in the SOS screen (stored locally on your device only; not uploaded to our servers).
  • Feedback: content of any message you choose to send via the in-app feedback feature.

2.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Device identifiers: anonymous installation ID used by Firebase to associate your data with your account.
  • App usage events: feature interactions (e.g., "PDF exported", "glucometer connected") collected via Firebase Analytics. These are anonymised and aggregated; they do not include the content of your health data.
  • Crash reports: stack traces and device model/OS version sent to Firebase Crashlytics when the app crashes, to allow us to fix bugs.
  • Subscription status: whether you are on the free tier, trial, or a paid subscription, managed via RevenueCat.

2.3 Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect precise GPS location (the SOS screen uses on-device location only to launch a local hospital search; location is never sent to our servers).
  • We do not collect contact list contents beyond what you explicitly add in the SOS screen.
  • We do not show advertisements and do not share data with advertising networks.
  • We do not sell your data to any third party.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • To provide the core app functionality: logging readings, calculating insulin doses, generating PDF reports, and displaying history and charts.
  • To synchronise your data across your devices when you are logged in.
  • To enable the caregiver mode, allowing a person you authorise to view (read-only) your health history.
  • To process and manage your subscription through RevenueCat and the relevant app store (Google Play or Apple App Store).
  • To send you medication reminders and alerts you have configured (via local notifications; we do not use third-party push-notification marketing).
  • To investigate and fix crashes and errors reported from your device.
  • To improve the app through anonymised aggregate analytics.
  • To respond to feedback or support requests you submit.

We do not use your health data to train artificial intelligence or machine learning models, and we do not share individual health records with any third party for commercial purposes.

7. Your Rights Under GDPR

As a resident of the European Union (or the UK), you have the following rights:

  • Right of access: you can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: you can correct inaccurate data directly in the app or by contacting us.
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): you can delete your account and all associated data at any time from the Profile → Settings screen. We will confirm deletion within 30 days.
  • Right to data portability: you can export your glucose history as CSV or PDF from the History screen. For a full structured export of all your data, contact us.
  • Right to restrict processing: you can ask us to restrict how we use your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to object: you can object to processing based on legitimate interest (e.g., analytics). We will stop unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent (especially health data), you may withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: you have the right to lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority. In Romania, this is the Autoritatea Națională de Supraveghere a Prelucrării Datelor cu Caracter Personal (ANSPDCP) — www.dataprotection.ro.

To exercise any right, email us at liviu.dev.cozac@proton.me. We will respond within 30 days (extendable to 3 months for complex requests, with notification).

8. Data Security

We take the security of your health data seriously and implement the following measures:

  • All data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS connections.
  • Data stored in Firebase Firestore is encrypted at rest by Google.
  • Firestore security rules restrict access so that each user can only read and write their own data.
  • Caregiver access is read-only, scoped to specific data collections, and requires an explicit code generated by the primary user.
  • Passwords are never stored in plain text; authentication is handled entirely by Firebase Auth.
  • Security PIN and biometric app lock options are available in Profile → Settings to prevent unauthorised access if your device is lost or shared.

No method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. If a data breach occurs that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours and inform affected users without undue delay.

9. Children's Privacy

DiabEasy is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

For users aged 16–18, we recommend that a parent or guardian review this Privacy Policy together with the user before account creation.

10. Cookies and Tracking

DiabEasy is a mobile application and does not use cookies. We do not embed web tracking pixels or third-party advertising SDKs.

Firebase Analytics uses an anonymous installation identifier (not linked to your identity) to aggregate usage statistics. You can opt out of analytics collection at any time in Profile → Settings → Analytics.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you by:

  • Displaying a notice within the app the next time you open it, and
  • Updating the "Effective date" at the top of this document.

Continued use of DiabEasy after notification of material changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not agree with the changes, you should delete your account before the changes take effect.
